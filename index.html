Pillar 1: True Representation

Your Voice. Your SGA. Your Future.

The Problem

The SGA currently operates in isolation. Officers only respond to specific emails (sometimes not even that), host senate meetings exclusively with organization presidents, and never engage with the general student body. No school in the US has such a disconnected SGA. Students have no platform to share their concerns about campus life or club performance.

My Solutions

  1. SGA Events with Students (Not Just Leaders): I will host regular SGA-sponsored events with FREE FOOD where ALL students—not just organization presidents—can come and share their thoughts on the current status of student organizations and campus life. These won't be boring meetings; they'll be casual, accessible gatherings where your voice actually matters.
  2. Student Reviews & Feedback System: We'll implement a comprehensive system to collect student reviews and emails about their experiences. This isn't just a suggestion box—it's a commitment to act on what you tell us. Every piece of feedback will be reviewed and addressed.
  3. SJNY Student Voice Newsletter: We're launching a student-run newsletter where YOU write the content. Students can submit articles about what they love, what needs to change, and what matters to them. The SGA eboard will evaluate submissions for appropriateness, but the voice will be 100% student-driven.
  4. Club Review Events & Monthly Voting: We'll host events where SGA connects with students randomly and asks for reviews of clubs. Plus, we're introducing Club of the Month voting—only students who attend the meeting can vote, making it authentic and engaged.

Together, Let's Build a Stronger SGA that hears you, sees you, and represents you.

Pillar 2: Resource Integrity

Transparent Budgets. Accountable Leaders. Maximum Impact.

The Problem

Budget allocation is opaque and unfair. Inactive organizations receive the same funding as high-performing clubs. Budget changes due to price increases are not communicated. Some eboard members leave mid-semester without consequences. The Food Pantry Pick and Go table lacks variety and student input.

My Solutions

  1. Streamlined Budget Allocation: We're overhauling the entire budget system. Budget bonuses for on-time budget submissions will be added immediately and clearly communicated. Most importantly, when budget changes occur due to price fluctuations, students WILL be informed.
  2. Performance-Based Funding Standards: Clubs must meet MINIMUM requirements to be considered active: a certain number of events per semester and minimum eboard member participation. Clubs that don't arrange any events but still receive semester funding are wasting student money.
  3. Additional Budget Request System & Collaboration Hub: Clubs planning major events should be able to request additional funds. If that's not possible, we'll act as a HUB to connect high-event clubs with low-event clubs for collaborative events so resources don't go to waste.
  4. Random Eboard Activeness Checks: We'll implement random checks on club eboards throughout the semester. Too many leaders disappear mid-semester, leaving clubs in chaos. We'll conduct performance reviews and gather feedback from club presidents.
  5. Food Pantry Expansion & Student Input: The Pick and Go table in the Student Life Office will be expanded with MORE food and DIVERSIFIED options based on student tastes. We'll add a QR code nearby where students can instantly suggest what kind of food they want.

Together, Let's Build a Stronger SGA that manages resources responsibly and rewards performance.

Pillar 3: Open Access

Remove Barriers. Create Opportunities. Empower Leaders.

The Problem

Freshmen cannot start clubs, key event-planning knowledge is gatekept, senate meetings conflict with student schedules, and there is no clear training pipeline for future SGA candidates.

My Solutions

  1. Freshmen Can Start Clubs: Starting immediately, freshmen will have the right to create and charter clubs. Freshman year is when students have the most spirit and energy, and they deserve the opportunity to lead from day one.
  2. Event Guide Video Tutorials: We'll create accessible, easy-to-follow video tutorials showing students exactly how to request an event, book rooms, and navigate SGA processes so this knowledge is public and permanent.
  3. Evening & Online Senate Meetings: We'll change Senate meeting requirements to include evening and online options. Students have their own events during Common Hour and shouldn't have to choose between participation and scheduling conflicts.
  4. SGA Sub-Executive Board Program: We'll create a Sub-Executive Board where students can be involved in SGA operations while participating in other clubs. This builds involvement, creates institutional knowledge, and prepares qualified future SGA candidates with real experience.

Together, Let's Build a Stronger SGA where everyone has access to leadership opportunities.
